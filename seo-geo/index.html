SEO & GEO Tag Generator — Free Meta Tags, Schema.org and llms.txt Generator

      
Generate all the tags your website needs to rank in Google and get cited by AI search engines. This tool covers traditional SEO (meta tags, Open Graph, Twitter Cards), modern structured data (Schema.org JSON-LD), and the cutting-edge GEO standard (Generative Engine Optimization) with llms.txt and AI crawler configuration. Everything runs in your browser — your data stays on your device.


      
What is SEO?

      
SEO (Search Engine Optimization) is the practice of optimizing your website to rank higher in search engines like Google. Key elements include meta title and description tags, Open Graph tags for social sharing, Twitter Cards, canonical URLs, and structured data. This tool generates all of these from a single form.


      
What is GEO (Generative Engine Optimization)?

      
GEO is a new optimization standard for AI-powered search engines — ChatGPT Search, Perplexity AI, Google AI Overview, and Bing Copilot. Instead of competing for 10 blue links, you compete for 2-7 citation slots in AI-generated answers. Key GEO tools include Schema.org structured data (especially FAQPage), the llms.txt file that tells AI about your site, and robots.txt configuration for AI crawlers.

Frequently Asked Questions

      

        
What is GEO and why does it matter?

        
GEO (Generative Engine Optimization) optimizes your content for AI search engines. Over 40% of search queries in 2026 are answered by AI. Without GEO optimization, your content may be invisible to ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, and Google AI Overview — even if you rank well in traditional Google results.

      

      

        
What is llms.txt?

        
llms.txt is a new standard (similar to robots.txt) that tells AI models about your website. It's a Markdown file placed at your site root (/llms.txt) containing your project name, description, and links to key content. AI crawlers use it to understand what your site offers.

      

      

        
How do I optimize my site for AI search engines?

        
Three key steps: 1) Add Schema.org structured data — especially FAQPage which has the highest AI citation rate. 2) Create an llms.txt file describing your site. 3) Configure robots.txt to block AI training crawlers but allow AI search crawlers. This tool generates all three.
