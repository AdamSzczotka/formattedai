Free HEIC Converter — Convert HEIC/HEIF Photos to JPG, PNG, AVIF Online

Convert iPhone and iPad photos to popular formats directly in your browser. HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's default photo format, but it's not supported by most apps on Windows and Android. Our converter changes HEIC/HEIF to JPG, PNG or modern AVIF — fast, free, and without uploading to any server. Your photos never leave your device.

How it works

  1. Drag HEIC/HEIF photos or click to browse — up to 20 files, max 50MB each
  2. Choose output format: JPG (universal), PNG (lossless) or AVIF (modern, small)
  3. Set quality using presets (Low 40, Medium 65, High 85, Maximum 100) or the slider
  4. Click Convert — download files individually or all at once as a ZIP archive

Features

  • Convert HEIC/HEIF to 3 formats: JPG, PNG, AVIF
  • Batch conversion — convert up to 20 photos at once
  • Quality presets: Low (40), Medium (65), High (85), Maximum (100)
  • Real-time progress bar with per-file status
  • Download individual files or batch as ZIP
  • Click-to-zoom image preview with transparency checkerboard
  • Works in all modern browsers via WebAssembly
  • 100% client-side — your files never leave your device

Frequently Asked Questions

What is HEIC format and why do I need to convert it?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is an image format used by default on Apple devices since iOS 11. It offers better compression than JPEG but is not supported by most browsers, image editors, and Windows/Android devices. Converting to JPG ensures universal compatibility.

How do I convert HEIC to JPG?

Drag your HEIC files into the converter, select JPG format, set quality (85 recommended for quality preservation), and click Convert. The tool processes everything in your browser via WebAssembly — no server upload needed. Download files individually or as a ZIP.

Does the HEIC conversion happen on a server?

No. All conversion happens locally in your browser using WebAssembly (WASM) technology. Your photos never leave your device — zero upload, zero server storage, full privacy.
